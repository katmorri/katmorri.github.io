Moog One Effects

In October 2018, Moog Music released the Moog One synthesizer, their first polyphonic analog synthesizer in over 35 years. Aside from its classic analog backbone, the Moog One features a handful of innovative digital audio effects. As a freelance DSP developer, I ported a couple of these effects from a high-level programming environment into a fast C++ framework. For this project, I wrote digital delay, mixing, panning, and other audio algorithms. While not included in the initial release of the Moog One, my code forms part of the company's library of real-time audio effects that may be used in future Moog products.
