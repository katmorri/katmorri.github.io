Razer Nari Ultimate

In September 2018, Razer announced the release of the Nari Ultimate, the world's first intelligent haptics-enabled gaming headset. The headset features physical modeling algorithms that I developed with Lofelt—a Berlin-based tech startup specializing in haptics for music and gaming—as part of my master's dissertation in Acoustics and Music Technology at the University of Edinburgh in Scotland.

Our physical modeling algorithms add an immersive three-dimensional quality to headphone audio that allows listeners to experience sounds as if they came from actual objects around them. Low-frequency vibrations—like the pounding of a kick drum or the thundering crash of a car— are converted into haptic feedback signals that oscillate and decay just like objects in the physical world.
