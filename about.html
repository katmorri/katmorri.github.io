About

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of California San Diego. As Ph.D. student, I take courses on audio signal processing and computer graphics, and I give lectures on video game music. My primary research interests lie in acoustic simulation, computer graphics, physical modeling, and machine learning. In addition to these topics, I'm also interested in questions relating to philosophy of science, aesthetics, and metaphysics.

Before coming to UC San Diego, I worked as an R&D Scientist at Applied Research in Acoustics and as a freelance developer at Moog Music and Lofelt. As a freelance developer, I wrote algorithms for music synthesizers and headphones, including the Razer Nari Ultimate headsets. As a master's student at Edinburgh and Wake Forest, I conducted research on topics in acoustics and optics. As an undergraduate at BYU, I researched topics in biophysics and philosophy. I'm originally from Los Angeles, California.
